# PM Communication: Influence and Align

PMs succeed or fail based on communication. This module covers internal alignment (stakeholders, presentations, writing) and external voice (positioning, content, media).

## When to Use This Module

- Preparing a presentation for leadership or board
- Writing memos, emails, or announcements
- Aligning stakeholders on a decision or direction
- Defining product positioning and messaging
- Planning content marketing or community building

## Workflow Overview

```
Understand Audience → Structure Message → Deliver → Follow Up
```

## Skills

### 1. Giving Presentations

**Context**: Presentations are decisions in disguise. Every presentation should end with a clear ask.

**Framework — Situation-Complication-Resolution (SCR)**:
1. **Situation**: What's the current state? (establish shared context, 1-2 slides)
2. **Complication**: What changed or what's at risk? (create urgency, 1-2 slides)
3. **Resolution**: What do you recommend? (your proposal + evidence, 3-5 slides)
4. **Ask**: What do you need from the audience? (decision, resources, alignment)

**Presentation Rules**:
- One idea per slide
- Lead with the conclusion, then support with evidence
- 10 slides, 20 minutes, 30pt font (Guy Kawasaki rule)
- Rehearse 3x — once for content, once for timing, once for energy

### 2. Written Communication

**Context**: Writing is the PM's primary tool for scaling influence. A well-written doc reaches everyone; a meeting reaches only attendees.

**Framework — Minto Pyramid Principle**:
1. **Start with the answer** (not the background)
2. **Group supporting arguments** (3-5 supporting points)
3. **Order logically**: by importance, chronologically, or by structure
4. Each paragraph opens with its conclusion

**Writing Checklist**:
- [ ] Can someone understand the key point from the first paragraph alone?
- [ ] Is every sentence necessary? (cut ruthlessly)
- [ ] Are you writing for the reader, not for yourself?
- [ ] Have you specified the ask or next step?

### 3. Stakeholder Alignment

**Context**: Misalignment is the #1 source of wasted effort. Align early and often.

**Framework — Stakeholder Map**:
1. **List stakeholders**: Who cares about this decision?
2. **Map influence vs interest**: High influence + high interest = manage closely
3. **Understand their success metric**: What does "good" look like to them?
4. **Pre-wire**: Share your proposal 1:1 before the group meeting. No surprises.
5. **Manage disagreement**: Acknowledge their concern, show you considered it, explain your trade-off

**Alignment Cadence**:
- **Weekly**: Direct team (standup or async update)
- **Biweekly**: Cross-functional partners (eng lead, design lead, data)
- **Monthly**: Leadership (progress against goals, blockers, asks)
- **Quarterly**: Broader org (strategy update, roadmap preview)

### 4. Running Offsites

**Context**: Offsites are for decisions and alignment that can't happen in regular meetings — strategy, culture, team bonding.

**Framework — Offsite Design**:
1. **Define the goal**: What decision or alignment must we leave with?
2. **Pre-read**: Send context 1 week before (not during the offsite)
3. **Structure**: Alternate between deep work sessions (60-90 min) and breaks
4. **Facilitation**: Designate a facilitator who isn't the decision-maker
5. **Output**: End with documented decisions, owners, and next steps

### 5. Media Relations

**Context**: Media amplifies your story to audiences you can't reach directly. Control the narrative.

**Framework**:
1. **Story, not features**: Media covers stories about people and change, not product specs
2. **Embargo strategy**: Give exclusive access to 1-2 outlets for bigger coverage
3. **Prepare talking points**: 3 key messages you want in every article
4. **Bridging**: When asked an off-topic question, bridge back: "What's really important here is..."
5. **Measure**: Track share of voice, not just mention count

### 6. Positioning & Messaging

**Context**: Positioning is how customers categorize your product in their mind. You don't control it — but you can influence it.

**Framework — Positioning Canvas** (April Dunford):
1. **Competitive alternatives**: What would customers use if you didn't exist?
2. **Unique attributes**: What do you have that alternatives don't?
3. **Value**: What capability do those attributes enable?
4. **Target segment**: Who cares most about that value?
5. **Market category**: What frame of reference helps customers understand what you are?

**Messaging Hierarchy**:
- **One-liner**: 10 words that explain what you do
- **Elevator pitch**: 30 seconds that make someone want to learn more
- **Key messages**: 3 supporting points that differentiate
- **Proof points**: Data, quotes, and case studies for each key message

### 7. Brand Storytelling

**Context**: Stories are how humans remember. A good brand story makes your product memorable and shareable.

**Framework — Story Structure**:
1. **Hero**: Your customer (not your company)
2. **Problem**: What they struggle with
3. **Guide**: Your product (you're the guide, not the hero)
4. **Plan**: How your product solves it
5. **Success**: What their life looks like after

### 8. Content Marketing

**Context**: Content marketing builds authority and generates inbound demand at scale.

**Framework — Content Strategy**:
1. **Topic authority**: Pick 3-5 topics where you have genuine expertise
2. **Content types**: Blog posts (awareness), guides (consideration), case studies (decision)
3. **Distribution**: SEO for evergreen, social for timely, email for nurture
4. **Cadence**: Consistency beats volume — 1 great post/week > 5 mediocre posts
5. **Measurement**: Track pipeline influenced, not just pageviews

### 9. Community Building

**Context**: Communities create a moat that competitors can't replicate. They take years to build and provide compounding returns.

**Framework — Community Flywheel**:
1. **Seed**: Start with 10 passionate users. Give them early access and make them feel special.
2. **Engage**: Create rituals (weekly threads, AMAs, meetups)
3. **Empower**: Give top contributors moderator status, recognition, insider access
4. **Scale**: Community generates content, support, and advocacy — reducing your CAC
5. **Measure**: Active contributors, response time, NPS, referral rate
