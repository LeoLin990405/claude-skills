# PM Team: Build and Lead People

Products are built by people. This module covers the full people lifecycle: hiring, developing, retaining, and leading teams — including sales team building for PM leaders who own revenue.

## When to Use This Module

- Writing job descriptions or designing interview loops
- Running effective 1:1s
- Having a difficult conversation
- Building team culture and rituals
- Setting up sales processes and compensation
- Navigating career transitions (your own or your reports')

## Workflow Overview

```
Hire → Onboard → Develop (1:1s, feedback) → Retain (culture, growth) → Lead (delegation, managing up)
```

## Skills

### Hiring Pipeline

#### 1. Writing Job Descriptions

**Context**: JDs are marketing documents for candidates, not internal req forms.

**Framework**:
1. **Open with impact**: What will this person accomplish in the first year? (not responsibilities)
2. **Requirements**: 5 max, distinguish must-have from nice-to-have
3. **Culture signal**: Show what it's actually like to work here (voice, values, team description)
4. **Avoid**: Jargon, unrealistic requirements, "rockstar/ninja", gendered language
5. **Include**: Salary range, location policy, interview process overview

#### 2. Conducting Interviews

**Framework — Structured Interviews**:
1. **Define the scorecard** before interviewing: 4-6 competencies, each with a rubric
2. **Ask the same questions** to every candidate (comparability)
3. **Behavioral questions**: "Tell me about a time when..." (past behavior predicts future behavior)
4. **Work samples**: Give a realistic exercise that mirrors the actual job
5. **Score independently** before debriefing with other interviewers (avoid anchoring)

#### 3. Evaluating Candidates

**Framework — Hire/No-Hire Signal**:
- Strong hire: Specific examples, admits mistakes, shows curiosity, asks great questions
- Weak signal: Generic answers, no ownership of failures, doesn't ask about the role
- Key question: "Would I be excited to work with this person every day?"

#### 4. Onboarding New Hires

**Framework — 30/60/90 Day Plan**:
- **Day 1-30**: Learn — meet the team, understand the product, read key docs, shadow meetings
- **Day 31-60**: Contribute — own a small project, ship something, build relationships
- **Day 61-90**: Lead — own a meaningful workstream, propose improvements, present at team meeting

### Team Development

#### 5. Running Effective 1:1s

**Context**: 1:1s are the most important meeting a manager has. They're for the report, not the manager.

**Framework**:
1. **Cadence**: Weekly, 30 min, never cancel (reschedule if needed)
2. **Agenda ownership**: Report owns the agenda. Manager adds items if needed.
3. **Structure**: Start with their topics → your topics → career/growth → action items
4. **Questions that work**: "What's blocking you?", "What would you do differently?", "What do you want to learn next?"
5. **Track**: Keep running notes and follow up on action items

**Template**: See [one-on-one-template](../../templates/one-on-one-template.md)

#### 6. Having Difficult Conversations

**Framework — SBI (Situation-Behavior-Impact)**:
1. **Situation**: "In yesterday's meeting with the client..."
2. **Behavior**: "...you interrupted the client three times during their presentation..."
3. **Impact**: "...which made them visibly frustrated and they cut the meeting short."
4. Follow with: "What's your perspective on what happened?"

**Rules**:
- Have the conversation within 48 hours (not weeks later)
- Private setting, never in front of others
- Focus on behavior, not character ("you did X" not "you are X")
- Listen to their side — you might have incomplete context

#### 7. Delegating Work

**Framework — Delegation Levels**:
1. **Tell**: "Do exactly this." (new hires, urgent/high-stakes)
2. **Sell**: "Here's what I'd recommend and why." (building trust)
3. **Consult**: "What do you think? I'll decide." (developing judgment)
4. **Agree**: "Let's decide together." (senior team members)
5. **Advise**: "Here's my input. You decide." (high-trust, experienced)
6. **Inquire**: "You decide. Tell me what you decided." (autonomous)
7. **Delegate**: "You decide. No need to tell me." (fully autonomous)

Match the level to the person's experience and the decision's stakes.

#### 8. Building Team Culture

**Framework**:
1. **Define values** as behaviors, not nouns ("we ship every week" not "excellence")
2. **Model the behavior** — culture is what the leader does, not what they say
3. **Hire for culture add** (not culture fit — fit creates homogeneity)
4. **Celebrate publicly**, correct privately
5. **Rituals**: Weekly wins, team lunches, post-mortems, hack days

#### 9. Designing Team Rituals

**Common Rituals**:
| Ritual | Purpose | Cadence |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Monday kickoff | Align on the week | Weekly, 15 min |
| Demo day | Show progress, celebrate | Weekly/biweekly, 30 min |
| Retrospective | Learn and improve | End of sprint, 60 min |
| Hack day | Creative exploration | Monthly, full day |
| Team lunch | Bond and decompress | Weekly |
| Wins channel | Recognition | Async, always-on |

### Career Navigation

#### 10. Managing Up

**Framework**:
1. Understand your manager's goals and metrics — align your work to what they care about
2. Bring solutions, not just problems
3. Give them no surprises — flag risks early
4. Make their job easier and they'll champion you
5. Adapt to their communication style (some want detail, some want headlines)

#### 11. Building a Promotion Case

**Framework**:
1. Understand the rubric for the next level (ask for it explicitly)
2. Start operating at the next level 6 months before seeking promotion
3. Document impact with metrics ("increased retention by 12%", not "worked on retention")
4. Build advocacy — your manager shouldn't be the only person who knows your work
5. Ask explicitly: "What would I need to demonstrate to be promoted to [level]?"

#### 12. Negotiating Offers

**Framework**:
1. Never give a number first — let them anchor
2. Negotiate the full package (base, equity, bonus, title, start date, remote policy)
3. Use competing offers as leverage, not threats
4. Be specific: "I was hoping for $X based on [market data / competing offer]"
5. Always negotiate — companies expect it. The worst outcome is they say no.

#### 13. Finding Mentors & Sponsors

**Distinction**:
- **Mentor**: Gives advice (you ask, they suggest)
- **Sponsor**: Uses their influence on your behalf (they advocate, you execute)

**How to find them**:
1. Mentors: Identify people 2-3 levels ahead. Ask specific questions, not "will you mentor me?"
2. Sponsors: Deliver exceptional work visibly. Sponsors choose you based on results.
3. Build the relationship first — mentorship/sponsorship follows trust

### Sales & Revenue

#### 14. Founder Sales

**Framework**:
1. Founders should sell the first 10-20 customers personally
2. Purpose: Learn objections, refine positioning, build customer relationships
3. Process: Cold outreach → Discovery call → Demo → Trial → Close
4. Document everything — you're building the sales playbook for future hires

#### 15. Building Sales Team

**Framework**:
1. Hire your first salesperson only after you've sold it yourself
2. First hire: player-coach who can sell and build process
3. Give them the playbook you built during founder sales
4. Measure ramp time: months to first closed deal (target: 3-4 months)
5. Don't hire the second rep until the first is consistently hitting quota

#### 16. Enterprise Sales

**Framework**:
1. Longer sales cycles (3-12 months) — plan accordingly
2. Multi-stakeholder: map the decision-making unit (champion, economic buyer, technical evaluator)
3. Security review, procurement, legal — build the "enterprise readiness" checklist
4. Land and expand: start with one team, prove value, then go org-wide

#### 17. Partnership & BD

**Framework**:
1. Strategic fit test: Does this partner help us reach customers we can't reach alone?
2. Start small: pilot with clear success metrics before long-term commitment
3. Assign an owner — partnerships die without active management
4. Review quarterly: is this partnership still strategic?

#### 18. Sales Compensation Design

**Framework**:
1. **OTE split**: 50/50 base/variable for AEs, 70/30 for SDRs, 60/40 for managers
2. **Quota**: 4-6x OTE for AEs (industry standard)
3. **Accelerators**: Pay more above quota to motivate over-performance
4. **Clawbacks**: Only for genuine fraud, not customer churn
5. **Keep it simple**: If a rep can't calculate their commission in 30 seconds, it's too complex

#### 19. Sales Qualification

**Framework — BANT**:
- **Budget**: Can they afford it?
- **Authority**: Can they make the decision?
- **Need**: Do they have the problem you solve?
- **Timeline**: When do they need a solution?

All four = qualified. Missing one = nurture. Missing two = disqualify.
