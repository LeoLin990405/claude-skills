## Interview Guide

### Warm-up (5 min)

> Thanks for taking the time. I'm researching [topic]. There are no right or wrong answers — I want to learn from your experience. I'll be recording so I can focus on our conversation. Is that okay?

1. Tell me about your role and what a typical day looks like.
2. How long have you been doing [relevant activity]?

### Context & Current Workflow (10 min)

3. Walk me through how you currently handle [task/problem area].
4. What tools or processes do you use?
5. **Tell me about the last time you [did the thing].** What happened step by step?
6. What's the most frustrating part of that process?

### Problem Exploration (15 min)

7. When you encounter [problem], what do you do?
8. How often does that happen? [Get frequency and severity]
9. What have you tried to solve it? [Existing workarounds]
10. **What happened as a result?** [Consequences of the problem]
11. If you could wave a magic wand and change one thing about [area], what would it be?
12. Why that one thing? [Dig into the "why"]

### Jobs-to-Be-Done (10 min)

13. When you [use current solution], what are you really trying to accomplish?
14. What does "success" look like for you in [this area]?
15. What would have to be true for you to switch to a different solution?
16. Who else is involved in this decision? [Decision-making unit]

### Wrap-up (5 min)

17. Is there anything I didn't ask about that you think is important?
18. Can I follow up if I have more questions?
19. Do you know anyone else who might be good to talk to about this?

### Interview Tips

- Listen 80%, talk 20%
- Follow the emotion — when they get animated, ask "tell me more about that"
- Never say "would you use X?" — ask about past behavior instead
- Silence is okay — let them think
- Ask "why?" or "tell me more" at least 5 times per interview
